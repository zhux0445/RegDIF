1 DIF Detection Methods

1.1 Likelihood Ratio Test

In likelihood ratio test, DIF will be tested one item at a time. The null hypothesis is that all but the anchor items are DIF. To test whether the jth item is DIF, the alternative hypothesis is that the anchor items and the jth item is DIF free, and all other items are DIF. If the null hypothesis is rejected (adjust p-value <0.05), the tested item will be flagged as DIF. Since we are testing several hypotheses, multiple comparisons need to be performed. The adjust p-values are the family-wise error rate. We assume the latent traits in reference and focal groups follow normal distributions. The mean and variance of reference group are fixed to be 0 and 1, respectively. The means and variances of focal groups can be freely estimated.
